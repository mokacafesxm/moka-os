#### Overall Impression

The browsing-and-cart half of `/commander` is genuinely well crafted: the design system is enforced in code (not just spec), the modal accessibility infrastructure (`useModalA11y`) is better than most production apps, and the coral-reserve discipline gives the UI real hierarchy. But the flow doesn't end anywhere — checkout is a placeholder — and several small consistency gaps (unlabeled bottom nav, two competing menu taxonomies, an unconfirmed "clear cart") sit right next to that craft. The single biggest opportunity: finish or gate the checkout step before anything else, because right now every well-designed screen upstream is spending trust that gets destroyed at the last moment.

#### What's Working

1. **`useModalA11y.js` is real accessibility infrastructure** — focus trap, Escape-to-close, and focus return, shared cleanly by `ProductPopup` and `LocationSheet`. The one place the app fully holds up for keyboard/screen-reader users.
2. **The Coral Reserve Rule is enforced in code, not just documented.** Coral appears only on primary CTAs, selected variant chips, the cart badge, and the active promo dot across every component checked — real hierarchy, not an accent-color free-for-all.
3. **`FreshnessIndicator.js`** is a small, domain-specific trust feature (menu "à l'instant / il y a X min" with a manual refresh) tied honestly to the real cache window in `getMenuData.js` — it directly addresses the real café failure mode of ordering something the counter just ran out of.

#### Priority Issues

**[P0] Checkout is a non-functional placeholder — the core task cannot be completed**
- **Why it matters:** PRODUCT.md defines success as "commander... payer sans confusion." No customer can currently pay. Every cart built ends at `checkout/page.js`'s "Bientôt disponible" screen, and by the peak-end rule this taints recall of the entire upstream experience.
- **Fix:** Ship a real minimal checkout (pickup slot + confirm), or change the cart CTA copy/state ("Continuer" implies near-completion) so customers don't invest effort in a cart they can't submit.
- **Suggested command:** `$impeccable shape`

**[P1] Bottom nav has zero accessible names on its 4 primary controls**
- **Why it matters:** Every other icon button in the codebase (`Favori`, `Fermer`, `Retirer/Ajouter un`) has an `aria-label`; `BottomNav`'s Home/Favorites/Cart/Account do not. Screen-reader users hit 4 unlabeled announcements on the control they'll use most, on every screen — and it breaks the project's own stated WCAG AA bar.
- **Fix:** Add `aria-label`s ("Accueil", "Favoris", "Panier", "Compte"); consider the same text-under-icon pattern `CategoryNav` already uses.
- **Suggested command:** `$impeccable audit`

**[P1] Category nav and homepage sections use two different taxonomies**
- **Why it matters:** The top nav's categories (from Notion) and the curated scroll sections (Popular/Matcha Lovers/etc., computed separately) disagree about what "the menu" is. Tapping a category opens a duplicate compact grid rather than jumping to a matching section — a direct hit to "match system/real world" and consistency. Dead scaffolding (`CategorySection`'s unused anchor `id`) suggests a deep-link feature was started and abandoned.
- **Fix:** Wire the category tap to scroll to the matching section anchor, or rename curated sections to align with the real category vocabulary.
- **Suggested command:** `$impeccable shape`

**[P2] "Vider le panier" has no confirmation or undo**
- **Why it matters:** One tap on a small text link wipes the entire cart with no confirm — on a shared tablet kiosk especially, a plausible mis-tap destroys a whole order with no recovery.
- **Fix:** Require a second-tap confirm, or visually separate/de-weight it further from the totals block.
- **Suggested command:** `$impeccable harden`

**[P3] TopBar social icons (and AccountView's account rows) are decorative dead ends**
- **Why it matters:** They visually match the tappable-icon language used everywhere else but do nothing on tap — the first thing a curious user might touch, in the first 2 seconds, is silently broken.
- **Fix:** Wire real links/handlers, or remove until ready.
- **Suggested command:** `$impeccable harden`

#### Persona Red Flags

**Casey (distracted mobile user):** The location-picker (a real pre-order decision) sits in the least thumb-reachable corner of the screen. `CartContext` resets on tab close by design (`sessionStorage`) — if her backgrounded mobile tab gets reclaimed mid-interruption, her cart silently vanishes with zero warning. She's also the persona hit hardest by the P0: she does the one-handed work of building a cart, then the checkout dead-end wastes all of it right when she was ready to commit.

**Riley (stress tester):** Typing a search query then tapping a category icon reveals `CategoryPanel` filters only by category, ignoring the active search query that the main feed respects — two browsing surfaces disagreeing about "filtered," from the same input, at the same time. Will find the unconfirmed "Vider le panier" within seconds and log it as a data-loss bug. On the checkout stub, the only exit doesn't return her to the cart she was reviewing.

**Jordan (confused first-timer):** Gets 4 unlabeled bottom-nav icons to guess-and-tap, unlike every other labeled icon row in the app. Taps the familiar social-icon row expecting MÖKA's socials; nothing happens. Hits the checkout "Bientôt disponible" wall after filling a cart, with no explanation of what to do next — reads as app failure, likely to abandon and walk to the counter unsure whether her order registered.

**Sam (accessibility-dependent):** `BottomNav`'s 4 focusable buttons have no accessible name at all — a hard navigation blocker via VoiceOver/NVDA on the primary controls. `Toast`'s add-to-cart confirmation has no `aria-live`. `SearchBar` relies on `placeholder` alone as a de facto label (a known WCAG anti-pattern). `CategoryNav`'s active state is conveyed only by a colored box-shadow ring, with no `aria-current`. Credit: both bottom sheets are genuinely usable for her via `useModalA11y`.

#### Minor Observations

- `CategorySection.js`'s unused anchor `id` looks like dead scaffolding for a deep-link feature — finish it (ties to the taxonomy P1) or remove it.
- `Dancing Script` loads on every page via `next/font/google` but is intentionally unused anywhere yet (confirmed) — a real font-download cost for zero current visual payoff.
- `PromoCarousel`'s `target="_blank"` links open a new tab from inside the ordering flow; on a kiosk this could strand the customer's browsing context.
- `AccountView`'s "Mes commandes"/"Mes informations"/"Favoris" rows render chevron affordances with no `onClick` — same dead-end family as the social icons.
- `variants.js`'s `priceFrom` silently falls back to `0` if Notion's price field is missing, rather than surfacing a content/data problem.
- Detector-confirmed: an undocumented `rgba(0,0,0,0.25)` icon drop-shadow at `BottomNav.js:60`, plus an equivalent undocumented `boxShadow: rgba(44,26,16,0.16)` at `BottomNav.js:103` that the detector's own regex misses (camelCase `boxShadow:` alone on a line isn't matched) — both are one-off literals that bypass `theme.js`, advisory-level but worth folding into the theme file next time `BottomNav` is touched.
- The placeholder-tan literal `#f0e4d4` is duplicated identically across 5 files (`ProductCard`, `ProductPopup`, `CartView`, `LocationSheet`, `CategoryPanel`) instead of referencing `MOKA.placeholderTan` from the shared theme — on-palette, but a missed dedup.

#### Questions to Consider

- If checkout genuinely isn't ready, should the cart CTA keep saying "Continuer" — implying near-completion — or should the flow be paused with an honest "bientôt disponible" framing before customers invest effort filling a cart they can't submit?
- The top category nav and the scrolling feed use two different taxonomies for "the menu" — which one does the business actually think in, and should the other be retired?
- Accessibility (`aria-label`) was applied to every icon button except the bottom nav and social icons — was it only applied to newer components, and would a checklist have caught this before shipping?
- Is "Vider le panier" really as low-stakes as clearing a search filter, or does erasing someone's whole order deserve the same friction as a destructive account action?
- Given the design system and modal-accessibility infrastructure are both genuinely solid, is the checkout gap a "not built yet" the team already knows about, or would it be more honest to gate `/commander` entry until the last step exists?
